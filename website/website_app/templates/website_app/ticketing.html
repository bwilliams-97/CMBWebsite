Charitable donations

We're delighted to have pledged to the What's a Pound? campaign this year. This means that when you apply for your tickets, there is the option of donating an additional £1 (or even £5) to our two chosen charities. Such a small donation can go a long way to helping people within our community here in Cambridge.

We would be extremely grateful if you could speak to your chosen guests about this campaign and whether they would like to support these worthy causes.

A description provided by each of our chosen charities is given below.

Red Balloon

Red Balloon supports young people who self-exclude from education as a result of severe bullying. Fostering a sense of community, Red Balloon combines academia, counselling and PSHE to help students overcome their trauma and re-engage with mainstream schooling. At our homely Cambridge centre, students can be found cooking, playing the piano, re-enacting Macbeth or even dissecting lambs’ hearts! Others, either too far or too frightened to attend the centre in person, take part in our Red Balloon of the Air programme, learning in an interactive classroom from the safety of their home. Students make impressive progress, both academically and personally, taking tiny steps towards the bright futures they deserve.

Wintercomfort

Wintercomfort provides vital welfare services, learning and development, volunteering and employment opportunities for homeless and vulnerably housed individuals in Cambridgeshire. Our centre, Overstream House is open 7 days a week offering breakfast, showers and laundry facilities as well as providing a wide programme of activities, information, advice and support. Wintercomfort was established in 1991. Homelessness is an increasing problem in Cambridge. In the last 12 months we have seen an increase of 12%. On a daily basis we are seeing up to 90 individuals per day. Here are a few other key statistics from 2016-17 about us:

  • We delivered 338 hours of guided learning
  • Supported over 763 individuals
  • 17 individuals achieved 1 Level 2 food safety qualification
  • 56 individuals gained new jobs

Application Process

Important! We are operating a ticket allocation system - tickets are NOT being allocated on a first-come-first-served basis. All ticket applications submitted will receive equal consideration.

Clare Priority Ticketing runs from 29th January, 7pm to 31st January, 7pm. All Clare students may apply for one personal ticket and up to two guest tickets. Every Clare student is guaranteed their own personal ticket; while we cannot guarantee all priority guest tickets, based on past numbers all Clare students who want up to two guest tickets have an extremely high chance of receiving them. Applications should be made via this ticketing website.

General Release Ticketing runs from 31st January, 7pm to 4th February, 7pm. All Clare students who have already applied for tickets during the Priority Ticketing window may apply for up to three additional guest tickets. Non-Clare University of Cambridge students may apply for one personal ticket and up to one guest ticket. Applications should be made via this ticketing website.

Fellows & Staff Ticketing runs from 29th January, 7pm to 4th February, 7pm. Clare College Fellows and Staff may apply for one personal ticket and up to one guest ticket. A free queue jump upgrade is included for all fellows, staff and their guests. Applications should be made via this ticketing website.

Alumni Ticketing runs until 15th February, 7pm. Clare College Alumni may apply for one personal ticket and up to one guest ticket. Applications should be made by email to alumni_ticketing@claremayball.com

Payment will normally be made by bank transfer. Emails requesting payment for all allocated tickets will be sent to students, Clare Fellows and Staff after General Release and Fellows & Staff Ticketing close on the 4th February. Emails requesting payment will be sent to Alumni after the Alumni ticketing window closes on the 15th February.
